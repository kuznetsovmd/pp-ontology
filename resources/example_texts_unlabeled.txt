The EOS token stand for End Of Sentence, and is used as a STOP token. I.E. when the model generate this token, it literally means the generation is done and should be stop. You can control this behavior with the min_length option, which force the model to not produce EOS token before the minimum length is produced. In your first try, after the model generated enough token, EOS token can be generated at any moment, even before reaching max_length. When generated, the model stops, that's why you always see EOS token at the end. That's normal behavior. As for your second try, it's weird indeed. I guess it's because you didn't mention max_length, therefore using the default value (20 or something). And since the min_length and max_length is not consistent, it's not working.
